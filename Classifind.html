CLASSIFIND: interface design mobile app

Classifind is a classified app for posting, selling, purchasing and shipping goods from private parties. The focus from the start was on generating customizable user feeds based on user parameters.

My Process

Project Overview

Challenge

Create a human-centered Classified app.

Goal

To provide relevant user feeds based on robust and flexible search parameters. As well as facilitate and simplify shipping services when required.

Wireframes

There is more than one way to create wireframes and ponder user flows. Figma and Balsamiq are great tools when looking to explore and test such ideas. For me however, there is no replacement for pen and paper. It’s probably just my nostalgia talking; I mean, when else do you get to use these scribe relics anymore! To be fair, I still think it’s the fastest and flexible ways to get your ideas down, so you can start sharing your key features and user flows with your team.

I find that starting on paper invariably speeds up my initial ideation process and without fail keeps me out of the business of making precise mid-fidelity wireframes that have major revisions I could have avoided all together.

Layouts & Spacing

Pixel perfect design ideally begins early in the layout process. Once I know what platforms and resolutions I’m designing for I like to start exploring different grid options after taking things like the apps responsiveness into consideration. Starting early in the wireframe process with grids and vertical baselines in place will lend your product consistency as it invariably gets more complex in the iteration process.

Native apps have guidelines that maintain things like minimum button sizes and spacing as well, and getting a grid in place early ensures your product is adhering to those guidelines.

Logo Iteration

The creative aspect of user interface design is what drew me to it. Logos marks, icons and style guides are often a user interface designer's time to let their creativity shine through. After a decade of experience in graphic design I've really come to relish this part of the process.

However, if studying user experience philosophies has taught me one thing it's this: if the design enhances the experience, only then is it doing what it's intended. As a user interface designer I associate a lot of value to the design, it's my job. However, if the design is cluttering the user experience in any way, it will need to be adjusted accordingly. As a designer, I'll be the first to admit that you can grow attached to your designs. However, if the user is struggling to navigate your design, they'll close the app and not open it again and no one will see your "beautiful" design anyway. Take it from me, don't put the cart before the horse!

Conclusion

Being the bargain bin hunter that I am, I’ve seen my fair share of classified apps. When tasked with overseeing the design and flow of one myself, I thought it would be easy to come up with a laundry list of features and patterns I had longed for when I was in the user’s shoes.

I soon realized upon starting the discovery process with this project that finding fault and being critical of a product design is a heck of a lot easier than coming up with a better solution yourself. This realization has informed my process when it comes to competitive analysis. Now when I look at similar products to one that I’m working on, I pay close attention to what does work, not what doesn’t. That’s because the logical place to start is with a “minimum viable product”. If we, as an AGILE team, working on a product can focus on a core concept that offers a true value to our users, then you truly have something!

Once that is established and the app has some traction with its users, then is the time to enrich the user experience with other features. In other words, keep it simple. This ensures your minimum viable product doesn’t get diluted by competing priorities and your minimum viable product remains the “mvp”. Once that is sorted out, you can revisit your” jobs to be done” list with all the knowledge of your users you’ve acquired and make an informed decision about what features will enrich the user experience.
